Story Scripting

Find a start point, Once you have decided what the style of your game is decide on a genre,. Look at other pieces of work in that genre like if it was horror you watch horror movies and read horror books. Create a character list. 3 stages to the list. Start - how do you want your characters to start out, what are their goals, morals, emotions, what is the purpose of the game? Middle - your characters are progressing, getting stronger, learning and developing and growing, how does this affect their morals, emotions? end - your characters should be fully developed (unless you have a sequel) they should be grown, and able to make decisions on their own, perhaps even have lost one of the other characters along the way, thus perhaps damaging them? Character development is very important in a good game, if a character doesn't develop then the game gets boring very quickly.

Plot Twists

“A game is as good as its plot twists” - Bill Gates. Portal’s cake Heavy Rain killer Alice in Detroit Spiderman Ending COD 4: Modern Warfare
You have to have a couple of good plot twist in a game to shock players and keep them entertained.
You keep adding little bits to the story to fill it up, although everything should contribute to either character development or story progression.

One Final Tip

One final tip. DO NOT USE story generators. Here are some examples why you shouldn’t use them.
When a Jedi from Porto Alegre learns to make tissue paper flowers, not everybody is supportive. However, his fortunes improve when his tutor becomes a vet. The Jedi realises he's actually a ghost.
When a fairy from Bolton decides to rob a bank, not everybody is supportive. However, her fortunes improve when her best friend buys a set of earplugs. It turns out the whole plot was a practical joke.
